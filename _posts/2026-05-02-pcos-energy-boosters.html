Introduction to PCOS and Energy Levels

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects millions of women worldwide. It is characterized by a range of symptoms, including weight gain, acne, and irregular menstrual cycles. One of the most debilitating symptoms of PCOS is fatigue and low energy levels. Women with PCOS often report feeling tired, sluggish, and lacking the energy to perform daily tasks.

Understanding the Causes of Fatigue in PCOS

The exact causes of fatigue in PCOS are not fully understood, but several factors are thought to contribute to it. These include insulin resistance, hormonal imbalances, and chronic inflammation. Insulin resistance, in particular, is a common feature of PCOS, and it can lead to fatigue, as the body's cells become less responsive to insulin.
